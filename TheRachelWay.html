The Horizon Workflow

Navigate to Horizon and open a chat. Here's the process:

  1. You can run out of tokens in Horizon and have to start a new chat — that's normal
  2. Start with a prompt that tells it who your presentation is for and your goal, along with the current outline
  3. Ask it to optimize your outline to accomplish your goals
  4. Iterate... until you run out of tokens
  5. Start a new conversation and keep going

Anthropological Observations

The "Local"

💻

"Local" = Your Laptop

Developers try to make fancy words for laptops. "Run it locally" just means "run it on your laptop." That's it.

📜

HTML: The Ancient Language

One of the earliest languages of the software engineer tribe. It runs the internet.

HTML is so basic it's one file. Their more complex languages require multiple files. We always do presentations in HTML because... easy.

📁 Create a folder on your laptop:

📁 my-presentation/
  ├─ presentation-outline.md ← from Horizon
  ├─ brand-guidelines.txt ← AI Friendly Brand Guide
  ├─ logo.png ← logos & relevant content
  └─ sample-presentation.html ← a sample HTML presentation

Upload everything Copilot needs to do its job. Think of it as packing supplies for the expedition.

The Main Event

Finally... GitHub Copilot

Open your folder in GitHub Copilot. Then drop this prompt:

"I have a big presentation to [audience]. I really want to impress them. Can you make an interactive presentation with some of the formatting of "sample-presentation.html" that uses the "brand-guidelines.txt" and covers the points in "presentation-outline.md""

Reference your files by name. Copilot reads them from your folder.
